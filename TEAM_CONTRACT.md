## 2. Git Process & Code Review Protocol

To ensure code repository integrity and prevent deployment pipeline failures, the team adheres to the following strict technical workflows:

- **Feature Branching:** No development is permitted directly on the `main` branch. All features, evaluations, and bug fixes must be developed on dedicated branches using the naming convention: `feature/[feature-name]`, `eval/[eval-task]`, or `fix/[bug-name]`.
- **Two-Teammate Approval Rule:** No Pull Request (PR) shall be merged into the `main` branch without explicit technical review and approval from at least **two (2) other teammates**.
- **No Solo Committing:** Merging personal PRs without review or bypassing the status checks is strictly prohibited. The author of the PR is **not** allowed to merge their own code; the merge must be executed by one of the reviewers after approvals are secured.
- **Continuous Integration (CI) Alignment:** Before opening a PR, the author must ensure that the local environment compiles successfully and that all core integration dependencies are updated.

## 3. Communication Windows & Boundaries

To maintain high cognitive performance and protect the team from burnout across the 10-day sprint, the following boundaries are established:

- **Active Communication Window:** The official hours for active collaboration and standard response times on Slack are between **9:00 AM** and **10:00 PM**.
- **Soft Stop Time:** A strict soft stop is enforced at **10:00 PM**. Any Slack message sent after this hour will be acknowledged and handled the following morning. No teammate is penalized or expected to respond during these hours.
- **Explicit Permission to Rest:** Teammates have the explicit right to declare an evening or a specific block off for recovery or family obligations, provided their current blocked tasks are communicated. The team's mandatory response is supportive confirmation.
- **Morning Productivity Alignment:** Acknowledging individual chronotypes, morning-focused production tracks will be respected to optimize deep-work coding blocks early in the day.

---

## 4. Work Plan & Board Update Rhythm

To guarantee full traceability and transparency of the project's velocity:

- **Real-time Board Sync:** The GitHub Project Board is the single source of truth for project status. Every teammate is required to update their respective cards across the states (`Backlog`, `In Progress`, `In Review`, `Done`) concurrently with the work being performed.
- **End-of-Day Shutdown Ritual:** At the end of each working day, every teammate must:
  1. Ensure their active branch is pushed to remote.
  2. Update their respective board cards.
  3. Post a single concise sentence in the team channel summarizing what was completed today and what will be picked up tomorrow.

---

## 5. Conflict Resolution & Support Plan

- **The 60-Minute Blocker Rule:** If a teammate faces a critical technical blocker (e.g., environment misconfiguration, debugging failure) and remains stuck for more than **60 minutes**, they must stop solo-debugging and flag the issue in the team channel for peer support or pair programming.
- **Technical Escalation Protocol:** If a technical blocker cannot be resolved by the team within **2 hours** of internal collaboration, it must be immediately escalated to the course Slack channel to request assistance from Instructors or TAs.
- **Engineering Design Disputes:** In the event of a disagreement regarding architectural choices or feature scoping, the team will vote based on technical feasibility and alignment with the core evaluation metrics. If a tie or deadlock occurs, the Lead Instructor will be consulted immediately for a final architectural decision.
