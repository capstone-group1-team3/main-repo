# Refund Policy

At our store we want you to shop with confidence, and that means being clear about when money comes back to you and how long it takes. This page explains refunds. If what you actually want is to send an item back, please read it together with our Return Policy, because in most cases a refund is the second half of a return — the return moves the goods, the refund moves the money.

## When you can ask for a refund

You may request a refund within **14 days** of the day your order was delivered. We count from the delivery date recorded on your order, not from the day you placed it, so a slow delivery never eats into your window. Requests made after the fourteenth day generally cannot be accepted as refunds, although if the problem is a genuine manufacturing fault you may still be covered for up to twelve months — see the Warranty Policy for that route, since a late fault is handled as a warranty claim rather than a refund.

If your item arrived damaged or defective, you are entitled to a full refund of the item price. We do ask for a **photo of the damage** before we approve this kind of refund; this is the "proof" step you will see referenced in your ticket, and until we receive it the request stays in a pending state. Photo proof is not required for every refund — for example a straightforward change of mind handled through a return does not need one — but for anything you describe as broken, damaged, or defective, the photo is mandatory and the request will not move forward without it.

## How long refunds take

Once a refund is approved, the time to see the money depends on how you paid. Refunds to a credit or debit card are processed within **5 business days**. If you paid using an offline bank payment slip, the refund takes longer — up to **10 business days** — because we have to collect your bank account details and issue a transfer rather than simply reversing a card charge. Vouchers and store credit are returned immediately once approved. These timelines start from approval, not from the moment you first opened the ticket, so the earlier you send any proof we ask for, the sooner the clock starts.

## Refunds and returns together

A common point of confusion: do you get your money back automatically when you return something? Not always at the same moment. For most returns the refund is issued after the returned goods are on their way back to us, which is why you may see the return approved first and the refund follow shortly after. For damaged goods we sometimes refund without asking for the item back at all, at our discretion, because it makes little sense to pay for return shipping on something that cannot be resold.

## Things that affect a refund

Shipping charges are refunded when the fault is ours — a defective item or the wrong item sent — but not when you simply changed your mind; in that case the original shipping stays with you, consistent with how return shipping is handled in the Return Policy. Orders paid across more than one method (for instance part voucher, part card) are refunded back to each method in proportion, so if this applies to you the refund may appear as two separate entries.

## Quick answers

*I was refused a refund because it had been more than two weeks — is that right?* Usually yes, the refund window is fourteen days from delivery, but ask us to check whether a warranty claim applies instead.

*Do I always need to send a photo?* Only when the item is damaged or defective. For other refunds a photo is not needed.

*Why is my refund taking ten days?* You most likely paid by bank slip, which is refunded by bank transfer and takes up to ten business days rather than the five days a card takes.
