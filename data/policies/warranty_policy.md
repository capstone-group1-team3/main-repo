# Warranty Policy

Our warranty protects you against manufacturing defects for a meaningful period after you receive an item. It is the route to use when a fault shows up later — after the shorter refund and return windows have closed — so think of it as the long tail of your protection rather than a replacement for those earlier options.

## What the warranty covers and for how long

Eligible products carry a **12-month** warranty against manufacturing defects, counted from the delivery date of your order. If a product develops a genuine fault through no misuse of your own within those twelve months, we will repair it, replace it, or in some cases refund it, at our discretion and depending on stock. Because the clock runs from delivery, the delivery date on your order is what determines whether a claim is still in time.

## What the warranty does not cover

A warranty is for defects, not for everything that can happen to a product. It does not cover accidental damage, misuse, or normal wear and tear. A screen cracked from a drop, water damage, or parts worn down through ordinary heavy use fall outside the warranty. This is the main difference between a warranty claim and a damaged-on-arrival refund: if the item arrived broken, that is a refund matter within fourteen days and needs a photo; if it worked and then failed from a defect months later, that is a warranty matter within twelve months.

## How a claim works

When you raise a warranty claim we will usually ask you to describe the fault and, as with damaged-item refunds, we may ask for a photo or short video so we can see the problem. The claim opens as a ticket and moves through the same lifecycle as our other requests — it starts open, may sit in a pending state while we wait for your proof, and is then either approved or rejected. An approved claim proceeds to repair, replacement, or refund.

## Warranty versus return versus refund

These three overlap in customers' minds, so to be clear: within fourteen days a damaged item is a **refund**; within thirty days an unwanted or wrong item is a **return**, and a defective one can be a **replacement**; and up to twelve months a defect that appears later is a **warranty** claim. The distinguishing factor for warranty is time — it is the option that remains once the earlier windows have closed.

## Quick answers

*My item broke after four months of normal use — am I covered?* Very likely yes, that is what the twelve-month warranty is for, provided it is a defect rather than accidental damage.

*I dropped it and the screen cracked — is that warranty?* No, accidental damage is not covered by the warranty.

*It arrived broken — is that a warranty claim?* No, that is a refund within fourteen days and needs a photo; the warranty is for faults that appear later.
