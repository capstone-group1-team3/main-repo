# Replacement & Payment Policy

This page covers two things that often come up together at the end of an order: swapping a faulty item for a working one, and the ways you can pay (and be paid back).

## Replacements

A replacement is for when you want the **same item**, working, rather than your money back. It runs on the same **30-day** window as a return, but it is narrower: replacements are offered for **defective or wrong items only**, not for a change of mind. If you ordered the right thing and it simply is not to your taste, that is a return, not a replacement.

Replacements are **like-for-like**. We send the same product you ordered; a replacement is not a way to upgrade to a different model or size. If the exact item is out of stock, we will discuss alternatives with you, which may include waiting for restock, choosing a different item, or moving to a refund instead. As with damaged-item refunds, we may ask for a photo of the fault before approving a replacement, and the request moves through the usual lifecycle — open, possibly pending while we wait for proof, then approved or rejected.

If a fault appears after the thirty-day replacement window, the replacement route is closed but the Warranty Policy still covers manufacturing defects for up to twelve months.

## Payment methods

You can pay by credit card, debit card, voucher, or bank payment slip. Credit card is the only method that supports paying in instalments. A single order may be paid using **more than one method** — a common example is covering part of the total with a voucher and the rest with a card — and when that happens each method is recorded separately against the order.

## How split payments are refunded

Because an order can be paid across several methods, a refund on such an order goes back to each method in proportion, so you may see more than one refund entry for a single order. This is expected behaviour, not a duplicate.

## Payment method and refund speed

The method you paid with also affects how quickly a refund reaches you. Card refunds are processed within five business days. Bank slip payments are refunded by bank transfer and take up to ten business days, because we need your account details to send the money rather than simply reversing a charge. The full refund timings are in the Refund Policy; the point to remember here is that an offline bank payment is always the slowest to come back.

## Quick answers

*Can I swap for a different model?* No, replacements are like-for-like for the same item. For something different, use a return and place a new order.

*Can I pay part by voucher and part by card?* Yes, and a refund would then be split back across both.

*Why did I get two refund entries?* Your order was paid with two methods, so the refund was returned to each in proportion.
