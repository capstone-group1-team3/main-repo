# Return Policy

This policy covers sending items back to us. Returns and refunds are related but not the same thing: a return is about the goods physically coming back, while the money side is described in the Refund Policy. In practice a return usually triggers a refund once we have the item on its way, so it helps to read both pages.

## Your return window

You have **30 days** from delivery to start a return. This is a longer window than the fourteen-day refund window on purpose — you get a full month to decide whether an item is right for you, and if within that month you want your money back for a damaged item specifically, the shorter refund rules still apply on top of this. Starting a return means opening a request and getting it approved; the item does not need to be back in our warehouse within thirty days, it just needs the request opened within that time.

## Condition of returned items

To be accepted, returned goods must be **unused and in their original packaging**, with any tags, manuals, and accessories included. We understand you may have opened the box to inspect an item — that is fine — but an item that shows signs of use, is missing parts, or comes back without its packaging may be refused or accepted only at a reduced refund. Hygiene-sensitive products and personalised items are the usual exceptions and generally cannot be returned once opened, even inside the thirty-day window.

## Who pays for return shipping

This depends entirely on the reason for the return, and it is the part customers most often overlook. If the item is **defective** or we sent you the **wrong item**, the company pays return shipping — the mistake is ours, so the cost is ours. If you are returning because you simply **changed your mind**, return shipping is paid by you, the customer. The same logic runs through the Refund Policy: original shipping is only refunded when the fault was ours.

## No restocking fee

We do not charge a restocking fee on standard returns. Some retailers deduct a percentage; we do not, so an approved return for a returnable item comes back to you in full for the item price, subject only to the shipping rule above.

## Returns that become replacements or warranty claims

If you want the same item rather than your money back, that is a replacement rather than a return — see the Replacement Policy, which runs on the same thirty-day window but is limited to defective or wrong items. And if a fault appears after the thirty days have passed, the return route is closed but the Warranty Policy may still help for up to twelve months from delivery.

## Quick answers

*How long do I have?* Thirty days from delivery to open the return.

*Do I pay to ship it back?* Only if you changed your mind. If it is defective or we sent the wrong thing, we pay.

*Is there a restocking fee?* No.

*I used the item a little — can I still return it?* Returned goods must be unused and in original packaging, so a used item may be refused or refunded only in part.
